Filipino Recipes & Step-by-Step Guides

  • Lechon:
    Ingredients:
    • 1 whole pig (cleaned and prepared)
    • Salt and pepper
    • Garlic, crushed
    • Onion, quartered
    • Lemongrass (tanglad)
    • Bay leaves
    • Soy sauce (optional, for basting)
    Steps:
    1. Rub the pig inside and out with salt and pepper.
    2. Stuff the belly with garlic, onion, lemongrass, and bay leaves.
    3. Sew the belly shut and skewer the pig on a large bamboo stick.
    4. Roast over charcoal, turning regularly, for 4–6 hours until skin is crispy and golden.
    5. Baste with soy sauce if desired for extra flavor and color.
    6. Serve hot with liver sauce or vinegar dip.
  • Halo-Halo:
    Ingredients:
    • Shaved ice
    • Evaporated milk
    • Sweetened banana (saba), jackfruit, red mung beans
    • Nata de coco, macapuno, kaong
    • Leche flan
    • Ube halaya (purple yam)
    • Pinipig or rice crispies (optional)
    • Ice cream (ube or vanilla)
    Steps:
    1. Layer the sweetened fruits, beans, and jellies at the bottom of a tall glass.
    2. Add a generous amount of shaved ice on top.
    3. Pour evaporated milk over the ice.
    4. Add leche flan, ube halaya, and ice cream.
    5. Top with pinipig or crispies if desired. Mix before eating.
  • Kare-Kare:
    Ingredients:
    • 1 kg oxtail or beef shank
    • Peanut butter (or ground roasted peanuts)
    • Annatto (achuete) for color
    • Eggplant, pechay (bok choy), string beans
    • Banana heart (optional)
    • Garlic and onion
    • Bagoong (fermented shrimp paste), for serving
    Steps:
    1. Boil oxtail or beef until tender. Set aside broth.
    2. In a pan, sauté garlic and onion. Add annatto for color.
    3. Pour in the broth and peanut butter. Stir until smooth.
    4. Add the boiled meat and simmer until sauce thickens.
    5. Blanch vegetables separately and arrange on top before serving.
    6. Serve hot with bagoong on the side.
  • Puto Bumbong:
    Ingredients:
    • Glutinous rice (pirurutong or purple variety)
    • Banana leaves for serving
    • Butter or margarine
    • Grated coconut
    • Brown sugar or muscovado
    Steps:
    1. Soak glutinous rice overnight, grind, and let dry until slightly damp.
    2. Pack the rice mixture into bamboo tubes (bumbong).
    3. Steam using a special puto bumbong steamer for about 10 minutes.
    4. Carefully remove and place on banana leaves.
    5. Spread with butter and top with grated coconut and brown sugar.
    6. Best served hot and fresh.
  • Bicol Express:
    Ingredients:
    • 500g pork belly, sliced
    • 2 cups coconut milk
    • 1 cup coconut cream
    • Garlic, onion, and ginger
    • Green and red chili peppers
    • Shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    Steps:
    1. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger in a pan.
    2. Add pork and cook until lightly browned.
    3. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
    4. Add shrimp paste and chili peppers. Stir well.
    5. Simmer until pork is tender and sauce is slightly reduced.
    6. Pour in coconut cream and cook for another 5–10 minutes.
    7. Serve hot with rice.

Where Food Meets Memory

Discover some of the most beloved and iconic Filipino foods!

Lechon Kawali
A crispy deep-fried pork belly dish, Lechon Kawali is a popular Filipino comfort food, often served with liver sauce. Its roots trace back to Spanish colonial times and festive celebrations.
Halo-Halo
A colorful shaved ice dessert with sweet beans, jellies, fruits, and leche flan. Halo-Halo means "mix-mix" and is a summer staple in the Philippines.
Kare-Kare
A rich peanut stew with oxtail, vegetables, and bagoong. Kare-Kare is believed to have originated from Pampanga and is a highlight of Filipino feasts.
Puto Bumbong
A purple rice cake steamed in bamboo tubes, Puto Bumbong is a Christmas delicacy enjoyed after Simbang Gabi (night mass).
Bicol Express
A spicy stew of pork, coconut milk, and chilies, Bicol Express hails from the Bicol region, famous for its love of spicy food.
Sinigang na Baboy
A sour pork soup made with tamarind, Sinigang is a classic Filipino comfort food, known for its refreshing and tangy broth.
Chicken Adobo
The unofficial national dish, Adobo is chicken braised in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices. Its origins predate Spanish colonization.
Pancit Canton
A stir-fried noodle dish with vegetables, meat, and seafood, Pancit Canton is inspired by Chinese cuisine and is a birthday staple.
Bibingka
A soft rice cake baked in clay pots and topped with salted egg and cheese, Bibingka is a Christmas favorite in the Philippines.
Lumpiang Shanghai
Filipino spring rolls filled with ground pork and vegetables, Lumpiang Shanghai is a party favorite and always present at gatherings.
Filipino Spaghetti
A sweet-style spaghetti with hotdogs and cheese, this dish is a Filipino adaptation of the Italian classic, loved by kids and adults alike.
Menudo
A tomato-based stew with pork, liver, potatoes, and carrots, Menudo is a staple at Filipino fiestas and family gatherings.
Turon
A sweet snack of caramelized banana and jackfruit wrapped in spring roll wrapper and fried to golden perfection.
Pochero
A hearty stew of beef, plantains, potatoes, and vegetables, Pochero is influenced by Spanish cocido and is a comfort food in many regions.
Ensaymada
A soft, sweet bread topped with butter, sugar, and cheese, Ensaymada is a Filipino adaptation of the Spanish ensaimada.
Cassava Cake
A moist cake made from grated cassava, coconut milk, and condensed milk, Cassava Cake is a popular merienda or dessert.

    Famous Places to Visit for Filipino Foods

  • La Loma, Quezon City – Known as the "Lechon Capital of the Philippines," La Loma is lined with legendary lechon houses like Elar's Lechon and Ping Ping's Lechon.
  • Razon’s of Guagua (Pampanga & Metro Manila) – Famous for their creamy Halo-Halo and Kapampangan delicacies.
  • Aristocrat Restaurant (Roxas Blvd, Manila) – A historic spot for classic Filipino dishes like Chicken Barbecue and Kare-Kare.
  • Milky Way Café (Makati) – Renowned for Kare-Kare, Pancit Luglug, and Halo-Halo.
  • Aling Lucing’s (Angeles City, Pampanga) – The birthplace of Sisig, a must-visit for foodies.
  • Binondo, Manila – The world’s oldest Chinatown, famous for Lumpiang Shanghai, Pancit Canton, and Filipino-Chinese fusion.
  • Dolor’s Kakanin (Malabon) – The go-to place for traditional Filipino rice cakes like Bibingka and Kakanin.
  • Legazpi City, Bicol – For authentic Bicol Express and spicy coconut-based dishes.
  • Via Mare (multiple branches) – Famous for Puto Bumbong, Bibingka, and other merienda classics.
  • Maty’s Tapsilog (Parañaque) – A legendary spot for Tapsilog and Filipino breakfast favorites.

Explore these places for an authentic taste of Filipino cuisine and culture!

Lechon Kawali Recipe

  2. Drain and let it dry completely.
  3. Deep fry until golden and crispy.
  4. Slice and serve with liver sauce.

Sinigang na Baboy Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Boil pork with tomatoes, onions, and tamarind until tender.
  2. Add vegetables like radish, eggplant, and string beans.
  3. Season with fish sauce and adjust sourness to taste.
  4. Serve hot with rice.

Chicken Adobo Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Marinate chicken in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns.
  2. Sauté garlic, then add chicken and marinade.
  3. Simmer until chicken is tender and sauce is reduced.
  4. Serve with steamed rice.

Pancit Canton Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Sauté garlic and onion, add pork, chicken, and shrimp.
  2. Add vegetables and stir-fry until crisp-tender.
  3. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, and broth.
  4. Add canton noodles and cook until sauce is absorbed.
  5. Serve hot with calamansi.

Bibingka Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Mix rice flour, coconut milk, sugar, and eggs into a batter.
  2. Pour into banana leaf-lined molds and bake until set.
  3. Top with salted egg and cheese, then bake until golden.
  4. Brush with butter and serve warm.

Lumpiang Shanghai Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Mix ground pork, carrots, onions, garlic, and seasonings.
  2. Wrap mixture in lumpia wrappers and seal edges.
  3. Deep fry until golden and crispy.
  4. Serve with sweet chili sauce.

Filipino Spaghetti Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Sauté garlic and onion, add ground pork and cook until browned.
  2. Add hotdogs, tomato sauce, banana ketchup, and sugar.
  3. Simmer until sauce thickens.
  4. Serve over spaghetti noodles with grated cheese.

Menudo Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Sauté garlic and onion, add pork and liver, cook until browned.
  2. Add tomato sauce, potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers.
  3. Simmer until vegetables are tender and sauce thickens.
  4. Serve hot with rice.

Turon Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Slice bananas and jackfruit, roll in sugar.
  2. Wrap in spring roll wrapper.
  3. Fry in hot oil until golden and crispy.
  4. Drain and serve as a snack or dessert.

Pochero Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Boil beef with onions, peppercorns, and salt until tender.
  2. Add plantains, potatoes, and vegetables.
  3. Simmer until all ingredients are cooked through.
  4. Serve with tomato sauce on the side.

Ensaymada Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Mix flour, sugar, yeast, eggs, and butter to form a dough.
  2. Let rise, then shape into coils and let rise again.
  3. Bake until golden, then brush with butter and top with sugar and cheese.
  4. Serve as a sweet snack or breakfast.

Cassava Cake Recipe

    Step-by-Step Process:
  1. Mix grated cassava, coconut milk, condensed milk, and sugar.
  2. Pour into a baking dish and bake until set.
  3. Top with a custard layer and bake until golden.
  4. Cool, slice, and serve as dessert.
